*Expand for tips on telling similar-looking scripts apart:*

{{% details "Arabic script" %}}

The script spans many countries, but a few added letters narrow it down:

- **Plain Arabic** вҶ’ the Arab world (no letters beyond Ш§вҖ“ЩҠ).
- **Persian (Farsi) вҶ’ Iran.** Adds four letters: Щҫ ЪҶ Ъҳ ЪҜ (p, ch, zh, g).
- **Urdu вҶ’ Pakistan.** Uses the Persian additions plus Щ№ ЪҲ Ъ‘ Ъә Ы’, and is usually written in the slanted, flowing **NastaКҝliq** style.

{{% /details %}}

{{% details "South Asian" %}}

All are Brahmic; sort them by the headline stroke along the top:

- **Headline present:** **Devanagari** (Hindi, Nepal) вҖ” straight bar, angular letters (аӨ… аӨҶ аӨ•); **Bengali** (Bangladesh, East India) вҖ” a headline too, but rounder with triangular, hooked shapes (аҰ… аҰҶ аҰ•).
- **No headline:** **Tamil** (Tamil Nadu, Sri Lanka) вҖ” angular with open loops (а®… а®Ҷ а®•); **Sinhala** (Sri Lanka) вҖ” extremely round and curly (а¶… а¶Ҷ а¶ҡ).
- **Sri Lanka** shows Sinhala and Tamil together (plus English).

{{% /details %}}

{{% details "Southeast Asian" %}}

Four distinct scripts, one country each:

- **Thai** вҖ” busy, with tall loops and flag-like ascenders; no spaces between words (аёҒ аёӮ аё„).
- **Lao** вҖ” like a rounder, simpler Thai with fewer letters and curvier shapes (аәҒ аәӮ аә„).
- **Khmer** вҖ” tall and spiky, with stacked subscript consonants hanging below the line (бһҖ бһҒ бһӮ).
- **Burmese** вҖ” almost all circles and arcs (бҖҖ бҖҒ бҖӮ).

{{% /details %}}

{{% details "East Asian (CJK)" %}}

Han characters (жјўеӯ—) are shared across the region, so look for the *extras*:

- **Kana вҶ’ Japan.** Any hiragana (гҒӮ гҒ® гӮ’) or katakana (гӮў гғҺ гғІ) mixed in means Japanese; Chinese never uses them.
- **Hangul вҶ’ South Korea.** Round, blocky syllables full of в—Ӣ and в–Ў shapes (н•ң көӯ м–ҙ); no Han needed.
- **Only Han, simplified вҶ’ mainland China.** Fewer strokes after the 1950s reforms (еӣҪ иҪҰ й—Ё и§Ғ).
- **Only Han, traditional вҶ’ Taiwan, Hong Kong, Macau.** More strokes (еңӢ и»Ҡ й–Җ иҰӢ); Hong Kong and Macau also carry lots of Latin signage (English / Portuguese).

{{% /details %}}
